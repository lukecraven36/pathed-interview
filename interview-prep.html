Technical cheat sheet

Everything you need to hold a credible AI conversation with a CTO. Click each platform tab. Remember: your job is to understand and advise — not to build. You're the most credible person in the sales conversation, not the most credible person in the server room.

The Foundation

Microsoft Azure

Microsoft's cloud computing platform — a global network of data centres that organisations rent access to rather than owning hardware. The analogy: Azure is the electricity grid. You don't build your own power station — you plug in and pay for what you use.

Core function

What Azure actually does

Stores data, runs applications, and hosts technology solutions without the customer owning physical servers. Pay-as-you-go model. Everything Simpson builds for customers — AI models, data pipelines, dashboards, RedactXpert — runs on Azure.

Why it matters here

Simpson's Azure specialisations

AI Platform on Azure, Build AI Apps on Azure, Analytics, Data Warehouse Migration, Enterprise App Migration. Four Solutions Partner designations: Data & AI, Digital & App Innovation, Infrastructure, Security.

Key phrase

"Inside your Azure tenant"

When Simpson talks about keeping customer data "inside your Azure tenant" — they mean the customer's data never leaves their own secure, organisation-owned slice of the Microsoft cloud. Critical for policing and NHS customers.

Your one-liner"Azure is the secure cloud foundation on which everything Simpson builds is deployed — it's the platform layer beneath Fabric, Azure OpenAI, Purview and the custom AI solutions."
The AI Engine

Azure OpenAI Service

Microsoft's secure, enterprise-grade version of the AI models that power ChatGPT (GPT-4, GPT-4o etc.), hosted inside a customer's own Azure environment. It's the raw AI reasoning engine — no interface, no buttons. Developers and data engineers point it at business problems.

Azure OpenAI vs ChatGPT

Same engine, different wrapper

ChatGPT is OpenAI's consumer product. Azure OpenAI is the same underlying models, but deployed securely inside Microsoft's enterprise cloud. Customer data stays inside their Azure tenancy — never used to train OpenAI's models.

vs Claude vs Gemini

All LLMs, different makers

ChatGPT (OpenAI/Microsoft), Claude (Anthropic — what you use here), Gemini (Google — what you used for the ETL Bot). Fundamentally the same category. Concepts like prompting, grounding, agents, hallucination are transferable across all three.

Simpson use

Built into their AI products

Azure OpenAI is the reasoning layer inside RedactXpert, the AI Front-Door product, and every Custom AI Solution Simpson builds. The "Build AI Apps on Microsoft Azure" specialisation specifically covers this capability.

Your one-liner"Azure OpenAI is the customisable AI foundation — the engine under the bonnet. Copilot is the finished product. Simpson uses both: Copilot where it fits, Azure OpenAI where something bespoke is needed."
The adoption stat lives here: Azure OpenAI growing 64% YoY, 85% Fortune 500 on Azure — but only 3.3% M365 users have paid Copilot seats. The infrastructure is being bought, the daily usage is the challenge.
The Data Platform

Microsoft Fabric

Microsoft's all-in-one modern data platform. Not just a data lake — it covers data storage (OneLake), engineering, pipelines, real-time analytics, Power BI, and has Copilot baked in throughout. Think of it as the entire data journey from ingestion to insight, unified.

Key component

OneLake

The storage layer within Fabric. A single logical data lake for the whole organisation — no more data silos. Data from different systems lands here. Direct Lake mode lets Power BI query it directly at speed, without copying data.

Why it matters

Simpson's biggest GTM motion

Simpson is a Fabric Featured Partner and Fabric Databases Featured Partner — recognised at launch. Mick Horne's team produces regular Fabric thought leadership. Many customers are mid-migration to Fabric now.

AI dimension

Copilot everywhere in Fabric

Copilot is woven through Fabric — for code review, documentation, pipeline generation, data exploration. Simpson positions this as "the first widespread use of generative AI across data engineering and analysis."

Your one-liner"Fabric is where the data lives, governed and ready. Azure OpenAI is what reasons over it. Power BI is what surfaces it. They work together — Fabric is the foundation that makes the AI trustworthy."
Your honest level: demo-level familiarity with Fabric and the Azure data platform. You're not a Fabric engineer. You understand the architecture and can discuss it credibly in presales. That's all the role needs.

The Governance Layer

Microsoft Purview

Microsoft's data governance, compliance and data security platform. Classifies data, tracks lineage, applies sensitivity labels, manages access, and creates an audit trail. For regulated customers like policing and NHS — it's non-negotiable. Simpson holds a Purview Implementation Partner designation.

Why it matters for AI

Responsible AI needs Purview

Before you can safely apply AI to data, you need to know what data you have, where it lives, who can access it, and how sensitive it is. Purview answers all four questions. It's the governance foundation that makes AI trustworthy — especially in regulated sectors.

Practical use

Sensitivity labels & lineage

Sensitivity labels (e.g. Confidential, Highly Sensitive) travel with data across SharePoint, Teams, emails, Fabric. Lineage shows where data originated and how it's been transformed. Audit trails are essential for police and NHS DPIA compliance.

Presales angle

The responsible AI unlock

In a Copilot Readiness Assessment, Purview is always part of the scope. "Do you have sensitivity labels in place? Is your SharePoint governed?" Without Purview foundations, Copilot is a data oversharing risk. With Purview, it's a productivity engine.

Your one-liner"Purview is what makes AI deployable in regulated environments. It's the difference between 'we want to use AI' and 'we're allowed to use AI.'"
The Direction of Travel

Agentic AI

The shift from AI that suggests or drafts to AI that acts — autonomously taking actions, calling tools, orchestrating workflows and making decisions within defined boundaries. The Beech Tree investment specifically funds this capability. Ashley wrote the blog on it.

The autonomy spectrum

5 levels of AI action

1. Information only (surfaces facts) → 2. Suggests (human chooses) → 3. Drafts (human reviews) → 4. Acts with review (human can override) → 5. Acts autonomously (no human in the loop). Most customers today are at 2–3. Agentic AI is 4–5.

Multi-agent pattern

Specialised agents + orchestrator

Multiple specialised agents (e.g. Transaction Sentry, Identity Verifier, Access Guardian) coordinated by an orchestrator that routes decisions based on confidence and impact. High-impact decisions get routed to a human adjudicator. This is the Meridian fraud detection architecture.

Built on

Azure OpenAI + Copilot Studio

Agent reasoning runs on Azure OpenAI. Agent orchestration, tool definitions, knowledge sources and escalation policies are configured in Copilot Studio. Fabric provides the real-time data context. Purview governs the data. AI Foundry handles evaluation and red-teaming.

The presales angleThe challenge with selling agentic AI is that customers need to trust it before they'll let it act. Your job is to design the governance guardrails — human-in-the-loop thresholds, audit trails, scope by design — that make autonomous action feel safe, not scary.
The Commercial Unlock

Responsible AI

Not a compliance overhead — a presales accelerator. In regulated sectors like policing, NHS and housing, a procurement-ready responsible AI annex inside the proposal is a deal accelerator. It also de-risks Simpson against reputational harm in sectors where one bad headline costs years of trust.

Microsoft's 6 principles

The framework to reference

Fairness · Reliability & Safety · Privacy & Security · Inclusiveness · Transparency · Accountability. Aligns with NIST AI RMF and the UK Government AI Playbook. This is the Microsoft Responsible AI Standard v2.

In a regulated proposal

Four things to make explicit

1. Where data lives and the Azure boundary. 2. How the model is grounded (RAG, citations, what hallucination controls exist). 3. Human-in-the-loop pattern for high-stakes decisions with audit trail. 4. Bias/drift testing approach and red-team prompts.

Policing/NHS overlay

Sector-specific requirements

DPIA, Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard, ICO guidance, FCA AI guidance (FS), College of Policing standards (blue light), NHS AI guidance. The SIRO/IG sign-off chain matters. Government AI Playbook is the UK policy anchor.

The key insight for the CTO interview"Responsible AI isn't the reason we do AI slowly — it's the reason customers buy AI confidently. In regulated sectors, the governance design is the commercial unlock."
