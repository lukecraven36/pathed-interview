The Foundation

Microsoft Azure

Microsoft's cloud computing platform — a global network of data centres that organisations rent access to rather than owning hardware. The analogy: Azure is the electricity grid. You don't build your own power station — you plug in and pay for what you use.

Core function

What Azure actually does

Stores data, runs applications, and hosts technology solutions without the customer owning physical servers. Pay-as-you-go model. Everything Simpson builds for customers — AI models, data pipelines, dashboards, RedactXpert — runs on Azure.

Why it matters here

Simpson's Azure specialisations

AI Platform on Azure, Build AI Apps on Azure, Analytics, Data Warehouse Migration, Enterprise App Migration. Four Solutions Partner designations: Data & AI, Digital & App Innovation, Infrastructure, Security.

The AI Engine

Azure OpenAI Service

Microsoft's secure, enterprise-grade version of the AI models that power ChatGPT (GPT-4, GPT-4o etc.), hosted inside a customer's own Azure environment. It's the raw AI reasoning engine — no interface, no buttons. Developers and data engineers point it at business problems.

The Governance Layer

Microsoft Purview

Microsoft's data governance, compliance and data security platform. Classifies data, tracks lineage, applies sensitivity labels, manages access, and creates an audit trail. For regulated customers like policing and NHS — it's non-negotiable. Simpson holds a Purview Implementation Partner designation.

Why it matters for AI

Responsible AI needs Purview

Before you can safely apply AI to data, you need to know what data you have, where it lives, who can access it, and how sensitive it is. Purview answers all four questions. It's the governance foundation that makes AI trustworthy — especially in regulated sectors.

Practical use

Sensitivity labels & lineage

Sensitivity labels (e.g. Confidential, Highly Sensitive) travel with data across SharePoint, Teams, emails, Fabric. Lineage shows where data originated and how it's been transformed. Audit trails are essential for police and NHS DPIA compliance.

The Direction of Travel

Agentic AI

The shift from AI that suggests or drafts to AI that acts — autonomously taking actions, calling tools, orchestrating workflows and making decisions within defined boundaries. The Beech Tree investment specifically funds this capability. Ashley wrote the blog on it.

The autonomy spectrum

5 levels of AI action

1. Information only (surfaces facts) → 2. Suggests (human chooses) → 3. Drafts (human reviews) → 4. Acts with review (human can override) → 5. Acts autonomously (no human in the loop). Most customers today are at 2–3. Agentic AI is 4–5.

Multi-agent pattern

Specialised agents + orchestrator

Multiple specialised agents (e.g. Transaction Sentry, Identity Verifier, Access Guardian) coordinated by an orchestrator that routes decisions based on confidence and impact. High-impact decisions get routed to a human adjudicator. This is the Meridian fraud detection architecture.

Built on

Azure OpenAI + Copilot Studio

Agent reasoning runs on Azure OpenAI. Agent orchestration, tool definitions, knowledge sources and escalation policies are configured in Copilot Studio. Fabric provides the real-time data context. Purview governs the data. AI Foundry handles evaluation and red-teaming.

The presales angleThe challenge with selling agentic AI is that customers need to trust it before they'll let it act. Your job is to design the governance guardrails — human-in-the-loop thresholds, audit trails, scope by design — that make autonomous action feel safe, not scary.
The Commercial Unlock

Responsible AI

Not a compliance overhead — a presales accelerator. In regulated sectors like policing, NHS and housing, a procurement-ready responsible AI annex inside the proposal is a deal accelerator. It also de-risks Simpson against reputational harm in sectors where one bad headline costs years of trust.

Microsoft's 6 principles

The framework to reference

Fairness · Reliability & Safety · Privacy & Security · Inclusiveness · Transparency · Accountability. Aligns with NIST AI RMF and the UK Government AI Playbook. This is the Microsoft Responsible AI Standard v2.

Quick Reference

AI Acronyms A–Z

The terms you'll hear in the room — from the CTO, from customers, and in Simpson's own content. Plain English, no jargon. Click any card for a fuller explanation.

Core AI concepts
LLM
Large Language Model
The AI brain behind ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini and Azure OpenAI.
A neural network trained on vast amounts of text that can understand and generate human language. GPT-4, Claude 3, Gemini — all LLMs. They predict the most likely next token given everything before it. Simpson's custom AI solutions are built on top of LLMs via Azure OpenAI.
RAG
Retrieval-Augmented Generation
How you give an AI access to your own documents and data.
Instead of relying only on what the model was trained on, RAG retrieves relevant chunks from a knowledge base (documents, databases, SharePoint) and feeds them into the prompt as context. This is how RedactXpert and the AI Front-Door "know" about the customer's own data. Prevents hallucination by grounding answers in real sources.
MCP
Model Context Protocol
A standard that lets AI models connect to external tools and data sources.
An open protocol (originally from Anthropic, now widely adopted) that defines how AI models talk to external systems — databases, APIs, file systems, calendars. Think of it as a universal plug socket for AI integrations. Claude in Claude.ai uses MCP to connect to things like GitHub, HubSpot and Xero. In agentic architectures, agents use MCP to call tools.
GPT
Generative Pre-trained Transformer
The model architecture behind OpenAI's models and Azure OpenAI.
The architecture underpinning OpenAI's models (GPT-3, GPT-4, GPT-4o). "Generative" = produces output. "Pre-trained" = trained on large text datasets before fine-tuning. "Transformer" = the neural network architecture. You don't need to understand the maths — just that GPT is OpenAI's model family, and Azure OpenAI gives enterprise-grade access to it.
SLM
Small Language Model
A lighter, faster, cheaper AI model for specific tasks.
A smaller, more focused model trained for a specific domain (e.g. legal documents, medical records, code). Cheaper to run, faster to respond, easier to deploy on-premise. Ashley's agentic AI blog mentions SLMs as part of the architecture — a specialised agent might use an SLM for a narrow task rather than a full LLM. Microsoft Phi is a popular SLM family.
NLP
Natural Language Processing
The older term for AI that understands and processes human language.
The field of AI concerned with understanding text and speech. Pre-LLM, NLP covered things like sentiment analysis, entity extraction, translation. LLMs have largely superseded traditional NLP for most tasks, but the term still appears in job descriptions and older documentation. If someone says "NLP capability" they broadly mean AI that understands language.
GenAI
Generative AI
AI that creates new content — text, images, code, data.
The category of AI (LLMs, image generators, code assistants) that produces new content rather than just classifying or predicting. ChatGPT, Copilot, Midjourney, GitHub Copilot are all GenAI. Distinct from predictive/discriminative AI (which classifies existing data). Most of Simpson's current AI proposition is GenAI-based.
ML
Machine Learning
AI that learns patterns from data to make predictions.
The broader field of algorithms that learn from data without being explicitly programmed. LLMs are a type of ML, but ML also covers classical approaches like regression, decision trees, random forests. Simpson's demand forecasting (boiler failure prediction, arrears risk) uses ML. Qlik AutoML lets you build ML models from your BI data without writing code.
Data & platform terms
ETL
Extract, Transform, Load
The process of moving data from source systems into a data platform.
Extract data from a source (a database, API, spreadsheet), Transform it (clean, reshape, join, aggregate), Load it into a destination (a data warehouse, lake, or Fabric OneLake). This is what your Gemini ETL Bot automated. In Simpson's world, Fabric pipelines, Databricks notebooks and Azure Data Factory all perform ETL. ELT (Extract, Load, Transform) is the modern variant — load raw data first, transform later.
API
Application Programming Interface
How software systems talk to each other.
A defined set of rules that lets one piece of software request services from another. When Pathed Hub sends a new client to FreeAgent, it calls FreeAgent's API. When Simpson's custom AI solution queries a customer's data, it often uses APIs. Azure OpenAI is itself accessed via API. REST APIs (using HTTP) are the most common in enterprise data work.
BI
Business Intelligence
Reporting and analytics — dashboards, KPIs, data visualisation.
The practice of using data to inform business decisions. Tools: Power BI, Qlik, Tableau, Cognos. The traditional BI layer is where Simpson started — and where most of their customer base still has day-to-day touchpoints. AI is now being layered on top of BI (Copilot in Power BI, Qlik AutoML) to move from descriptive (what happened) to predictive (what will happen) and prescriptive (what should we do).
SaaS
Software as a Service
Software delivered via the cloud, subscription-based.
Software hosted in the cloud and accessed via browser or API — you pay a subscription rather than a one-off licence. RedactXpert is moving toward a SaaS model. Microsoft Fabric, Qlik Cloud and Azure OpenAI are all SaaS/PaaS. The shift from on-premise to SaaS is often the trigger for Simpson's engagement — customers migrating to Azure and needing their data landscape rebuilt.
MLOps
Machine Learning Operations
The engineering discipline of deploying and maintaining AI models in production.
Like DevOps for software but for ML models. Covers: model versioning, experiment tracking, deployment pipelines, monitoring, drift detection, retraining. Without MLOps, a model that works in a pilot often degrades silently in production. Azure ML, Databricks MLflow and Azure AI Foundry are the key MLOps platforms. Simpson's Responsible AI Lead and MLOps Engineer roles map directly to this.
RBAC
Role-Based Access Control
Who can see what data, based on their job role.
A security model where data access is defined by job role rather than individual user. Essential in regulated environments — a social worker should see their own caseload, not every child in the county. In AI deployments, RBAC determines what data an AI agent can query. Purview enforces RBAC across the Microsoft data estate. Comes up constantly in policing and NHS engagements.
DPIA
Data Protection Impact Assessment
The formal risk assessment required before processing sensitive data with AI.
A GDPR requirement for any high-risk data processing activity — which almost every AI deployment involving personal data qualifies as. The DPIA documents what data is processed, why, what the risks are and how they're mitigated. Simpson's Responsible AI Lead conducts or supports DPIAs on every regulated AI engagement. Mentioning this in the interview shows you understand the compliance reality.
PII
Personally Identifiable Information
Any data that can identify a specific person.
Names, addresses, email addresses, NHS numbers, police record numbers, financial account details. The whole point of RedactXpert is automatically removing PII from documents before disclosure. In AI deployments, knowing where PII is (Purview) and ensuring it's not accidentally included in model training or RAG retrieval is a core governance requirement. Special Category PII (health, race, religion) carries a higher bar under GDPR.
Architecture & agentic terms
PoV
Proof of Value
A time-boxed build that proves an AI use case delivers real value.
Not a demo — a real, working version of the solution tested against real data in a controlled environment. A PoV de-risks the full investment by proving the technology works in the customer's context before committing to production. Usually 6–10 weeks. Simpson's standard AI engagement motion: Assessment → Readiness → PoV → Build → Scale. Almost every Simpson AI engagement includes a PoV phase.
PoC
Proof of Concept
A quick technical demonstration that a concept is feasible.
Similar to PoV but typically earlier-stage, less rigorous, and more about technical feasibility than commercial value. A PoC says "this can work." A PoV says "this delivers value in our context." In practice the terms are often used interchangeably, but if you use "PoV" in a Simpson context you're using their preferred language and signalling you understand the value-first framing.
CoE
Centre of Excellence
A central team that owns AI standards, platforms and best practice.
The organisational pattern for governing AI at scale. A CoE owns the AI policy, manages the central platform (Azure OpenAI, Copilot Studio), sets standards for responsible AI, builds reusable assets, and supports business units running their own use cases. Recommending a CoE model is part of Simpson's AI Operating Model advisory offer. You'd recommend this to Meridian Building Society.
ROI
Return on Investment
What the business gets back relative to what it spends.
For AI engagements, ROI typically comes from: time saved × hourly rate, fraud/error losses avoided, faster processing reducing headcount growth, or revenue uplift from better decisions. The Meridian example: £208k investment returning £2.4m annually = 5-month payback. Always frame ROI conservatively (lower bound) and name the assumptions — it's more credible and protects Simpson from overpromising.
NPV
Net Present Value
The total value of future returns in today's money.
A financial metric that accounts for the time value of money — £1 today is worth more than £1 in three years. The Meridian 3-year NPV of £5.8m means that all future cash flows (discounted for time) add up to £5.8m of value in today's terms. You don't need to calculate it — just understand it's the "total commercial case" number that CFOs and investment committees want to see.
KYC
Know Your Customer
The identity verification process financial services firms must follow.
A regulatory requirement for banks and building societies to verify who their customers are, assess their risk profile and monitor for money laundering. In the Meridian fraud detection use case, the Identity Verifier agent handles KYC checks. AI can dramatically accelerate KYC by automating document review, matching identities and flagging anomalies — reducing what can be a days-long manual process to seconds.
RFP / RFI
Request for Proposal / Request for Information
Formal documents a customer sends when they want to buy something.
An RFI asks "tell us about your capability." An RFP asks "give us a priced proposal for this specific requirement." Responding to RFPs and RFIs is explicitly called out in the JD as a core presales activity. The Presales AI Specialist's job is to lead the AI-specific sections — solution design, responsible AI approach, architecture, team, timeline. Quality of RFP response is often the deciding factor in a public sector procurement.
SoW
Statement of Work
The contract document that defines what will be delivered, when and for how much.
The commercial document that formalises a consulting engagement — scope, deliverables, timeline, roles, assumptions, exclusions, price. You've built this into Pathed Hub. In Simpson's world, the Presales AI Specialist contributes to SoW scoping — defining what's in, what's out, what success looks like, and what the customer needs to provide. Simpson-specific & sector terms
FCA
Financial Conduct Authority
The UK regulator for financial services firms.
Regulates banks, insurers, building societies and investment firms in the UK. Has published guidance on AI and Big Data, Consumer Duty (2023 — requires firms to demonstrate good customer outcomes) and operational resilience. Any AI deployment at a firm like Meridian Building Society would need to be defensible to the FCA. Consumer Duty specifically requires firms to show AI decisions don't cause customer harm.
ICO
Information Commissioner's Office
The UK data protection regulator — enforces GDPR.
The UK regulator for data protection and privacy, enforcing GDPR and the UK Data Protection Act. Any AI system processing personal data needs to be ICO-compliant — hence DPIAs, sensitivity labels, data minimisation and purpose limitation. The ICO has published guidance specifically on AI and automated decision-making. Simpson's blue light and local government customers are particularly focused on ICO compliance.
PDS
Police Digital Service
The shared technology and procurement body for UK policing.
A national body that helps UK police forces buy and use technology. Simpson's RedactXpert is on the PDS Redaction Tool Framework — meaning any UK police force can procure it directly without running a full tender. Being on a PDS framework is a major commercial asset in the policing sector. Also relevant: the College of Policing publishes guidance on AI use in law enforcement.
OSCAR
Online System for Central Accounting and Reporting
HM Treasury's financial reporting system — a Simpson client.
The UK government's central accounting and reporting platform, used across Whitehall departments. HM Treasury appointed Simpson in April 2025 to support and improve OSCAR — a significant central government win. Demonstrates Simpson's capability in sensitive, high-visibility government data work. Useful context if the conversation turns to central government opportunities.
ATU
Account Technology Unit
Microsoft's internal team that manages major enterprise accounts.
Microsoft's field sales organisation. The ATU manages relationships with large enterprise customers. For Simpson as a partner, the relevant contacts are the ATU (who own customer relationships), the SSP (Specialist Sales Professional — the AI/Azure specialist) and the PDM (Partner Development Manager — who manages Simpson's relationship with Microsoft). Building named relationships with these people is how co-sell actually happens.
GTM
Go-To-Market
The strategy for how a product or service reaches customers.
Covers: who you're selling to (ICP — Ideal Customer Profile), what you're selling (the proposition), how you reach them (channels — direct, Microsoft co-sell, events, inbound), what you say (messaging), and how you prove it (references, case studies). The Presales AI Specialist role is explicitly described as supporting Simpson's AI go-to-market. Building the AI Assessment offer into a repeatable GTM motion is a core part of the job.
ICP
Ideal Customer Profile
A description of the type of organisation most likely to buy and succeed.
For Simpson's AI practice, the ICP is probably: UK public sector or regulated commercial, 500+ employees, existing Microsoft/Azure investment, a named CDO or Head of Data, a strategic AI mandate from the board, and a data estate that's messy enough to need help but mature enough to build on. Knowing your ICP helps qualify faster — and disqualify faster, which is equally valuable.
TOEX
Tackling Organised Exploitation
A national UK policing programme — a major Simpson reference.
A national policing programme using data and analytics to tackle organised criminal exploitation — including county lines drug networks, modern slavery and VAWG (Violence Against Women and Girls). Simpson built the data infrastructure and analytics capability underpinning it. A Detective Chief Superintendent called the work "revolutionary." Referenced across multiple police forces. Shows the mission-critical, national-scale work Simpson can deliver in the blue light sector.
